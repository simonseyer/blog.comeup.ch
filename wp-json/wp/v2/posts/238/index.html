Rule No. 1: there is no plan. There are no must-sees for us or a bucket list that we want to tick off. We also don\u2019t aim to see as many countries as possible or try to optimize for the best blog content (sorry!).<\/p>\n\n\n\n

With the opportunity of having a full year of travel time available to us, we don\u2019t want to limit ourselves to our imagination. We want to take spontaneous decisions, stay at places that we like, and leave the ones we don\u2019t. We want to follow the advice of locals and get inspired by the places we are instead of looking too far ahead.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

That said, we of course got some ideas that will guide us. I\u2019m writing this post on the plane to Panama City where our journey will start in about 10 hours. We aim to keep this the only flight of the trip until we head back. Which implies that we will be \u201climited\u201d to Central and South America. I\u2019m putting limited in quotes because it\u2019s a vast and diverse continent where we have no doubt we could spend well more than a year.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
